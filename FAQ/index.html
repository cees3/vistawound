Frequently Asked Questions

01Do you accept Medicare for in-home wound care?

Yes. We accept Medicare, most Medicaid plans, and many commercial insurance carriers. Our team will verify your coverage and benefits before beginning care to ensure transparency.

02 What kinds of wounds do you treat?

We specialize in chronic and complex wounds , including diabetic foot ulcers, pressure injuries (bedsores), venous and arterial ulcers, post-operative wounds, trauma wounds, skin tears, and infected or non-healing wounds.

03Where do you provide care?

We treat patients directly in their homes, assisted living facilities, and skilled nursing settings across Northeast Oklahoma. We bring advanced wound care directly to you—no travel required.

04What specific areas of Oklahoma do you serve?

We serve Northeast Oklahoma, including Tulsa, Broken Arrow, Claremore, Muskogee, Bartlesville, and surrounding communities. Contact us to check if we cover your specific area.

05How quickly can a new patient be seen?

In many cases, we can schedule an evaluation within 1 to 2 business days of receiving a referral or request. We prioritize urgent cases whenever possible to prevent complications.

06Why choose mobile wound care over a traditional clinic?

We’re mobile, meaning we bring expert care to you, eliminating travel challenges and delays. But we’re also built on integrity and follow-through. We don’t just show up—we communicate, document, and deliver high-quality, compassionate care with professionalism.

07Do you offer advanced treatments like skin grafts or wound vacs?

Yes. Our providers are trained in advanced wound care, including skin substitute grafting, negative pressure wound therapy (NPWT), and bedside debridement when medically appropriate.

08Do I need a referral to receive care?

Not necessarily. Patients and families can contact us directly to request a consultation. However, we often work with referrals from primary care providers,home health nurses ,case managers, and hospital discharge planners.

09Who will be coming to my home?

One of our licensed wound care providers —either a physician or a nurse practitioner—will visit you. Our entire team is experienced, professional, and committed to respectful, compassionate care.

10What happens during a visit?

We’ll assess your wound, review your medical history, and provide treatment on the spot if needed. We’ll also create a personalized care plan and communicate with your other providers (like yourprimary doctor or home health nurse).

11How do you work with my other providers?

We believe in strong communication. We share timely updates and documentation with your care team—whether it’s your primary care provider, specialist,home health agency, or facility staff We prioritize clarity and collaboration.

12How does Vista reduce hospital readmissions?

By delivering timely, expert care in the home or facility setting, we prevent wound deterioration, infections, and avoidable ER visits. We manage early complications and escalate care appropriately when needed, keeping patients safe at home.

13What should I have ready for my first visit?

Please have your insurance card, a current list of your medications, and any relevant medical history available. If someone helps care for you, they are welcome to be present during the visit.

14Is your care compliant with HIPAA and privacy standards?

Absolutely. We follow all HIPAA regulations to protect patient privacy, and we use secure methods to share medical records and communicate with referring providers.
